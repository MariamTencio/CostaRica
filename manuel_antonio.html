About Manuel Antonio

Established in 1972 to protect the vibrant forests and pristine beaches, Manuel Antonio is a sanctuary for many of Costa Rica's endangered species. Though it’s the smallest of the country’s national parks, covering nearly 1,700 acres of land (683 hectares) and 136,000 acres of ocean (55,000 hectares), it is widely considered one of Costa Rica's top destinations.
